Our Mission

Stories Should Speak to Everyone

StoryCast was founded on a simple belief: storytelling is a universal human right. We're building the most accessible platform for audio and video stories on the web.

Our Mission

Traditional media platforms often treat accessibility as an afterthought. We believe it should be the foundation.

Every story published on StoryCast meets rigorous accessibility standards before it ever reaches an audience. This means:

  • Content is designed for screen readers from day one
  • Transcripts are created alongside the media, not after
  • Visual descriptions are written by the creators themselves
  • Keyboard navigation is tested throughout development

Who We Serve

Our primary audience includes:

  • Deaf and hard-of-hearing users who need captions and transcripts
  • Blind and low-vision users who rely on audio descriptions and screen readers
  • Motor-impaired users who navigate by keyboard or switch
  • Cognitively diverse users who benefit from clear structure and multiple formats
  • Everyone else who simply prefers reading, watching with sound off, or learning in multiple modes

Accessibility Features

How to Use This Site

  • Skip Links: Press Tab on any page to reveal a "Skip to main content" link that bypasses navigation.
  • Keyboard Navigation: All interactive elements (buttons, links, media controls) are fully operable with Tab, Enter, Space, and arrow keys.
  • Transcripts: Every audio story has a full text transcript. Click "Show Transcript" on any story page to reveal synchronized text with timestamps.
  • Captions: All video content includes synchronized closed captions. Toggle captions using the CC button in the video player.
  • Audio Descriptions: Video stories include audio description tracks that narrate visual elements. Select "AD" in the video player.
  • Screen Reader Optimized: Semantic HTML5 landmarks, ARIA labels, and heading hierarchies ensure smooth navigation with assistive technology.
  • Reduced Motion: If you prefer minimal animation, enable "Reduce Motion" in your system preferences and our interface will adapt automatically.
  • High Contrast: The site supports Windows High Contrast mode. All interactive elements remain visible and functional.
  • Text Sizing: All text scales with your browser's zoom settings. The layout adapts to your preferred text size without breaking.
  • Focus Indicators: A bright amber focus ring appears around any element you navigate to with keyboard, ensuring you always know where you are.

Technical Standards

StoryCast is built to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards across all pages. We test regularly with:

  • NVDA and JAWS screen readers (Windows)
  • VoiceOver (macOS and iOS)
  • TalkBack (Android)
  • Keyboard-only navigation
  • Automated testing tools (axe, WAVE, Lighthouse)
  • User testing with disabled community members
